L I S A - L E S L I E

Triple threat; Lisa Leslie was an All-Star basketball player, Olympic gold medalist, and WNBA league MVP.

The Beginning

1972 - Lisa Leslie was born on a hot summer day in Los Angeles. With two sisters and one brother, Leslie quickly learned how to help take care of everyone after her father left home when she was a baby. Leslie’s mother, Christine, stood over 6 feet 3 inches tall. So when Leslie hit 6 feet tall when she was in middle school, she was quickly recruited to join the basketball team. Reluctantly, she obliged, and the team went undefeated. In eighth grade, she transferred to a junior high school without a girls' basketball team, and joined a boys' basketball team. Her success there contributed to her confidence, and Leslie soon began to get serious about basketball.

The Fame

At 14, before Leslie had even started high school, she had received more than 100 college recruiting letters. Continuing her takeover, she was her high school team's leading scorer and rebounder and led them to the California state championship during her Junior and Senior year. Between those two years, she was invited to participate on the USA's Junior World Championship team. Going into college, Leslie was the top recruit in the country. In her four years at USC, she set the conference record for scoring, rebounding, and blocked shots. Capping out her college career with the Naismith College Player of the Year award, Leslie goes down in history as one of the greatest to ever play at USC. In 2001, she was the first WNBA player to win the *Regular Season MVP, the *All-Star Game MVP and the *Playoff MVP in the same season. Lisa also led the Los Angeles Sparks to two back-to-back WNBA Championships (2001, 2002). Lisa won 4 Olympic Gold medals and was the first woman in the WNBA to make a slam-dunk during an official game.

The Legacy

The rise of a female powerhouse basketball player, coupled with the 90’s media takeoff, meant that Lisa Leslie largely influenced the creation and the success of the WNBA. Being one of the league’s first and best players sits high at the top of Leslie’s resume. For girls growing up in the NBA-only era, Leslie was a refreshing voice for young girls who loved the game. Seeing a female slam dunk was like realizing a dream was within reach. Leslie didn’t just set personal records, she invited all women to the table of professional basketball.

  • *Regular Season MVP: Awarded to the athlete who consistently played the best throughout the entire season.
  • *All-Star Game MVP: Awarded for the best perfomance in the WNBA All-Star Game.
  • *Playoff MVP: Awarded to the athlete who played the best during the Playoffs.
